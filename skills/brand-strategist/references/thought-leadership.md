# Thought Leadership

Create signature ideas that establish you as an authority.

---

## What Makes a Thought Leader

Thought leaders don't just share information - they:

1. **Have a unique perspective** on their field
2. **Create original frameworks** others use
3. **Challenge accepted wisdom** with evidence
4. **Predict trends** before they happen
5. **Shape conversations** in their industry

## The Contrarian Positioning Strategy

### Finding Your Contrarian Angle

**Exercise: The "Everyone Says" Audit**

List 10 common beliefs in your industry:
```
Everyone says: [COMMON BELIEF]
I believe: [YOUR TAKE]
Because: [EVIDENCE/REASONING]
```

**Examples:**
```
Everyone says: "Content is king"
I believe: "Distribution is king"
Because: "Great content with no audience is just a diary"

Everyone says: "Hustle 24/7"
I believe: "Strategic rest beats grinding"
Because: "My best ideas come during downtime"

Everyone says: "Follow best practices"
I believe: "Best practices = average results"
Because: "If everyone does it, there's no competitive advantage"
```

### The Contrarian Formula

```
"Most people think [X], but [Y] because [Z]."
```

**Variations:**
- "The problem with [common advice] is..."
- "What [experts] won't tell you about..."
- "I stopped [common practice] and [result]"

### Rules for Contrarian Takes

1. **Be genuinely contrarian** - Not just controversial for clicks
2. **Back it up** - Have experience or data
3. **Provide alternative** - Don't just tear down
4. **Pick your battles** - 1-2 core contrarian takes, not everything

## Creating Signature Frameworks

### What is a Signature Framework?

A named, repeatable process that solves a specific problem.

**Famous examples:**
- The 5 AM Club (Robin Sharma)
- The Lean Startup (Eric Ries)
- SPIN Selling (Neil Rackham)
- The 4-Hour Workweek (Tim Ferriss)
- Getting Things Done - GTD (David Allen)

### Framework Creation Process

**Step 1: Identify Your Repeated Process**

What do you teach or do repeatedly?
- How do you onboard clients?
- What's your creative process?
- How do you solve [specific problem]?

**Step 2: Break It Into Steps**

Optimal: 3-5 steps (memorable, actionable)

```
What I do:
1. [First step]
2. [Second step]
3. [Third step]
4. [Fourth step]
```

**Step 3: Name It**

**Naming patterns:**
- Acronym: SMART goals, SWOT analysis
- Number: 7 Habits, 5 Love Languages
- Metaphor: Blue Ocean Strategy, The Flywheel
- Adjective + Noun: Lean Startup, Growth Hacking
- Your Name + Method: The [Your Name] Method

**Step 4: Create Visual Representation**

- Circular (cycle)
- Linear (steps)
- Pyramid (hierarchy)
- Matrix (2x2 grid)
- Venn diagram (overlap)

**Step 5: Teach It Consistently**

Use the framework in:
- Blog posts
- LinkedIn content
- Presentations
- Client work
- Book/course

## Developing Original Ideas

### The Idea Generation System

**1. Daily Capture**
Note observations, frustrations, questions

**2. Weekly Synthesis**
Review notes, find patterns

**3. Monthly Deep Dive**
Develop 1-2 ideas into full concepts

### Sources of Original Ideas

| Source | Question to Ask |
|--------|-----------------|
| Your experience | "What worked for me that's different?" |
| Client patterns | "What do I see repeatedly?" |
| Industry gaps | "What's nobody talking about?" |
| Adjacent fields | "What can I borrow from [field]?" |
| First principles | "Why do we actually do this?" |

### The "Your Take" Formula

For any topic, ask:
```
1. What's the conventional wisdom?
2. Where is it wrong or incomplete?
3. What's a better approach?
4. What evidence supports my view?
5. What's the memorable way to say this?
```

## Building Authority Over Time

### The Authority Ladder

```
Level 1: Consumer → You learn
Level 2: Curator → You share others' ideas
Level 3: Commentator → You add perspective to ideas
Level 4: Creator → You create original ideas
Level 5: Category → You define a new category
```

### Authority Building Activities

| Activity | Authority Impact |
|----------|-----------------|
| Blog posts | Low (unless viral) |
| Guest podcasts | Medium |
| Speaking | Medium-High |
| Original research | High |
| Book | High |
| Named framework adopted by others | Very High |

### The Repetition Principle

Say your core ideas:
- More times than feels comfortable
- In different formats
- To different audiences
- Over a longer period

"If you're not sick of saying it, you haven't said it enough."
