# Discovery Question Bank

## Opening Questions

### Rapport Building (2-3 min)
- "Thanks for making time. Before we dive in, I saw [interesting thing about company]. Tell me about that."
- "How did you end up in your current role?"
- "What's keeping you busy these days?"

### Setting Agenda
- "Here's what I'd like to cover today: understand your current situation, identify what's working and what's not, and if there's a fit, discuss next steps. Sound good?"
- "I have some questions to understand your needs. Feel free to interrupt if anything isn't clear."

---

## Current State Questions

### Process Understanding
1. "Walk me through how [process] works today, start to finish."
2. "Who's involved at each step?"
3. "What tools or systems do you use?"
4. "How do you know when something goes wrong?"
5. "What happens when [key person] is out?"

### Volume & Frequency
6. "How many [items] do you process per [week/month]?"
7. "Is that consistent or does it vary? What causes spikes?"
8. "How long does each one take?"
9. "How much time total does the team spend on this?"

### Current Solutions
10. "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"
11. "Are you using any tools for this today?"
12. "What's working well that we shouldn't change?"

---

## Pain Discovery Questions

### Surface Level
13. "What's the most frustrating part of this process?"
14. "If you could fix one thing about [process], what would it be?"
15. "What would make your life easier?"

### Deeper Probing
16. "Tell me more about that. What makes it frustrating?"
17. "When did you first notice this was a problem?"
18. "How often does this happen?"
19. "What do you do when it happens?"

### Impact Questions
20. "What happens when [problem] occurs?"
21. "Who gets affected? How do they react?"
22. "What's the cost of this problem? Time? Money? Reputation?"
23. "What's the worst case if this isn't fixed?"

### Emotional Questions
24. "How does dealing with this make you feel?"
25. "What would it mean to your team to have this solved?"
26. "Imagine it's six months from now and this is fixed. What's different?"

---

## Quantification Questions

### Time
27. "How many hours per week does the team spend on this?"
28. "What percentage of someone's time goes to this?"
29. "If this was automated, what would they do instead?"

### Money
30. "What does an error here cost you?"
31. "Have you ever calculated what this problem costs annually?"
32. "What would you pay to make this problem go away?"

### Risk
33. "What happens if someone makes a mistake?"
34. "What's the compliance/legal risk here?"
35. "How does this affect customer satisfaction?"

### Opportunity
36. "What opportunities are you missing because of this?"
37. "What would you do with the extra time/resources?"
38. "How would solving this affect revenue/growth?"

---

## Decision Process Questions

### Authority
39. "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
40. "What's your role in the buying process?"
41. "Have you bought solutions like this before? How did that go?"

### Budget
42. "Do you have budget allocated for solving this?"
43. "How do decisions like this typically get funded?"
44. "What would make this a no-brainer investment?"

### Timeline
45. "When do you need this solved by?"
46. "What's driving that timeline?"
47. "What happens if you don't solve this by [date]?"

### Criteria
48. "What would success look like?"
49. "What's most important to you in a solution?"
50. "What would make you say no?"

### Competition
51. "Are you looking at other solutions?"
52. "What alternatives have you considered?"
53. "What would make us stand out?"

---

## Objection Prevention Questions

### Risk Concerns
54. "What concerns you about making a change?"
55. "What would need to be true for you to feel confident in this?"
56. "Have you had bad experiences with [vendors/automation] before?"

### Implementation
57. "How does your team handle change?"
58. "Who would be involved in implementation?"
59. "What could derail this project?"

### Stakeholders
60. "Who might push back on this? Why?"
61. "What do other stakeholders care about?"
62. "How can we make this easier to approve?"

---

## Closing Questions

### Interest Check
63. "Based on what we've discussed, do you see value in exploring this further?"
64. "On a scale of 1-10, how important is solving this?"
65. "What questions do you have for me?"

### Next Steps
66. "What would you need to see to move forward?"
67. "Who else should be in our next conversation?"
68. "What's the best way to follow up?"

### Timeline
69. "If we could deliver exactly what you need, how quickly could you move?"
70. "What would prevent you from starting this month?"

## Vertical-Specific Questions

### E-commerce
- "How do you handle multi-channel inventory?"
- "What's your process for order fulfillment?"
- "How do you deal with returns and exchanges?"

### Professional Services
- "How do you track billable time?"
- "What's your client intake process?"
- "How do you manage project deadlines?"

### Logistics
- "How do customers track their deliveries?"
- "What happens when a delivery fails?"
- "How do you communicate with drivers?"

### Legal
- "How do you manage document versions?"
- "What's your deadline tracking system?"
- "How do clients see case status?"

### Finance
- "How long is your month-end close?"
- "How do you handle reconciliation?"
- "What's your audit prep process?"

---

## Follow-Up Probes

Use these after any answer:
- "Can you tell me more about that?"
- "What do you mean by [term they used]?"
- "Why is that important to you?"
- "What else?"
- "And then what happens?"
- "How does that make you feel?"
- "What would [stakeholder] say about that?"
- "If I asked your team, what would they tell me?"

---

## Red Flag Detection Questions

### Budget
- "What happens if the price is higher than expected?"
- "Who approves spending over $X?"

### Authority
- "If you wanted to move forward today, could you?"
- "What's the typical approval process?"

### Need
- "On a scale of 1-10, how urgent is this?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this?"

### Fit
- "What would make this not work for you?"
- "What's your biggest concern about automation?"
