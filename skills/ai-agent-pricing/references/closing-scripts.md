# Closing Scripts

## Objection Responses

### "It's too expensive"

**Reframe to value:**
> "I understand. Let's look at it differently. You mentioned spending [X] hours per week on this. At your team's rate, that's $[Y] per month. Our solution is $[Z]. So you're actually saving $[Y-Z] every month. The question isn't whether you can afford it - it's whether you can afford not to."

**Breakdown tactic:**
> "That's $[X] per month, which is about $[X/30] per day. You told me this task costs you [Y] hours daily. Is $[X/30] worth getting those hours back?"

**Compare alternatives:**
> "What's the alternative? Hire someone at $[salary]? Build it yourself for [months] of engineering time? This is the fastest, cheapest path to solving the problem."

### "I need to think about it"

**Isolate the concern:**
> "Absolutely, take your time. To help me help you - what specifically are you thinking about? Is it the price, the timeline, or something else?"

**Create a timeline:**
> "Of course. When do you think you'll have a decision? I want to make sure I'm available for any questions."

**Offer a smaller step:**
> "What if we started with just [smallest tier]? That gives you a chance to see results before committing to more."

### "I need to check with my partner/team"

**Join the conversation:**
> "That makes sense. Would it help if I joined a quick call with them? I can answer any technical questions directly."

**Arm them with materials:**
> "Happy to help. What would be most useful for that conversation - an ROI breakdown? A one-pager they can review? A recorded demo?"

**Set the next step:**
> "When's that conversation happening? Let's schedule a follow-up call right after so I can address any concerns while they're fresh."

### "We'll build it ourselves"

**Calculate the true cost:**
> "I get it - you've got a great team. Let's do the math: A developer costs $[X]/month. This would take at least [Y] months to build properly. That's $[X×Y] plus ongoing maintenance. We're offering a working solution for $[Z] - that's [comparison]× cheaper and ready now."

**Highlight hidden complexity:**
> "There's more to it than the initial build. Error handling, edge cases, integrations, updates, monitoring... We've already solved all of that. Your team's time is better spent on your core product."

**Offer middle ground:**
> "What if we handle the automation and your team focuses on customizations? You get the best of both worlds."

### "I found a cheaper competitor"

**Understand the difference:**
> "Good to shop around. What are they offering? ... Interesting. Here's what you might want to ask them about: [specific capability they lack]. That's included in our solution."

**Total cost of ownership:**
> "The sticker price is one thing. But factor in: implementation time, support quality, reliability, and what happens when you need to scale. We've seen clients switch to us after the 'cheap' option cost them more in downtime and headaches."

**Stand firm on value:**
> "We're not the cheapest - and that's intentional. We're the solution that actually works. What matters more to you: saving $[X] now or solving the problem permanently?"

### "We don't have budget right now"

**Find the money:**
> "I hear that. But this automation saves $[X] per month. Where does that money currently go? If we can shift budget from [waste area], this pays for itself."

**Start smaller:**
> "What if we started with a pilot project? $[small amount] to prove the value. Once you see the ROI, budget becomes much easier to justify."

**Create urgency:**
> "Budget cycles reset when? Let's plan for that. In the meantime, every month is costing you $[X] in lost efficiency. Want me to send a business case you can use internally?"

### "Not a priority right now"

**Surface the pain:**
> "Understood. Out of curiosity, how much time does your team spend on [problem] each week? [Response] And that's been going on for how long? That's a lot of hours. What would have to happen for this to become a priority?"

**Future-pace:**
> "Where do you want to be in 6 months? If this keeps up, where will you actually be? The gap between those is what we solve."

**Plant the seed:**
> "Totally get it. Let me send you a quick case study of how [similar company] used this. When priorities shift, you'll have it ready. Can I check back in [X] weeks?"

## Closing Techniques

### The Assumptive Close
> "Great, let's get you started. I'll send over the agreement today. Do you prefer to pay monthly or get the annual discount?"

### The Summary Close
> "So to recap: You're losing [X] hours per week, which costs $[Y] monthly. Our solution fixes that for $[Z]. You'll see positive ROI in [A] days. Any reason not to move forward?"

### The Alternative Close
> "Would you prefer to start with the Growth plan, or does Scale make more sense given your volume?"

### The Now-or-Never Close
> "I can hold this pricing through [date]. After that, it goes up [X]%. Should I lock this in for you?"

### The Puppy Dog Close
> "Tell you what - try it for 14 days. If it doesn't work for you, cancel before then and pay nothing. Fair?"

## Discovery Questions

### Pain Discovery
- "What's the most tedious part of your day?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing, what would it be?"
- "What happens when this task doesn't get done?"

### Cost Discovery
- "How many hours per week goes into this?"
- "What's the cost when things go wrong?"
- "What could your team accomplish if this was automated?"

### Timeline Discovery
- "How long has this been a problem?"
- "What made you look for a solution now?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this in the next [X] months?"

### Budget Discovery
- "What would solving this be worth to you?"
- "Have you budgeted for a solution?"
- "What have you spent on trying to fix this before?"

### Decision Discovery
- "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
- "What's your process for bringing in new tools?"
- "What would make this a no-brainer for you?"

```

```

## Negotiation Principles

### Never Discount Without Getting Something
- Annual commitment → 20% off
- Case study permission → 10% off
- Referral commitment → 15% off
- Prepayment → 10% off

### Trade, Don't Give Away
> "I can lower the monthly by $100 if we go with an annual commitment. Does that work?"

### Walk Away Power
> "I understand this might not be the right fit. Not everyone values [what you offer]. Should we table this?"

### Silence is Your Friend
After stating price, stop talking. Let them respond first.
