# Sales Proposal Agent Prompts

Use these prompts with the `customer-sales-automation:sales-automator` agent for sales materials.

---

## Full Proposal Generation

```
Generate a proposal for:

**Client:** [Company Name]
**Contact:** [Name, Title]
**Industry:** [Vertical]
**Company size:** [employees / revenue range]

**Pain points identified:**
1. [Pain point 1] - Costs them $[X]/month
2. [Pain point 2] - Costs them $[X]/month
3. [Pain point 3] - Costs them $[X]/month

**Recommended solution:**
- [Workflow 1]: [Brief description]
- [Workflow 2]: [Brief description]
- [Workflow 3]: [Brief description]

**Budget signals:** [What they indicated]
**Timeline:** [When they need it]
**Competition:** [Who else they're talking to]

Include:
- Executive summary (2 sentences max)
- Problem statement using their words
- Solution overview with benefits
- ROI calculation with payback period
- 3 pricing tiers (good/better/best)
- Implementation timeline
- Social proof (relevant case study reference)
- Call to action with deadline
```

## Cold Outreach Email

```
Draft a cold email:

**Target:** [Role] at [Industry] companies
**Company size:** [employee range]
**Pain point to address:** [specific problem]
**Our solution:** [what we offer]

Tone: [professional / conversational / bold]
Length: [short / medium] - max [X] words

Email structure:
1. Hook: Reference something specific
2. Problem: Agitate the pain point
3. Solution: Brief mention of how we help
4. Proof: One stat or quick case reference
5. CTA: Low-commitment ask

Also generate:
- 3 subject line options (A/B test)
- 2 follow-up email variations (Day 3, Day 7)
```

## Follow-Up Email Sequence

```
Create a follow-up sequence after:

**Context:** [Initial meeting / proposal sent / demo / etc.]
**Client:** [Name] at [Company]
**What we discussed:** [Key points]
**Their objections/concerns:** [List any]
**Next step they agreed to:** [If any]

Generate:
1. **Immediate follow-up** (same day)
   - Thank them
   - Recap key points
   - Attach relevant resource

2. **Value add** (Day 2-3)
   - Share relevant case study
   - Or: Answer a question they had
   - Soft reminder of next step

3. **Check-in** (Day 5-7)
   - Ask if they had questions
   - Offer to clarify anything
   - Restate timeline/urgency

4. **Final nudge** (Day 10-14)
   - Acknowledge they're busy
   - One last value point
   - Easy out or reschedule option
```

## Objection Response Scripts

```
Generate response scripts for:

**Objection:** "[Exact objection client gave]"
**Context:** [What we're selling, at what price]
**Client type:** [Role, company size, industry]

Provide:
1. Acknowledgment (validate their concern)
2. Clarifying question (understand root cause)
3. Reframe (shift perspective)
4. Evidence (proof point or story)
5. Bridge (back to value)
6. Close (next step)

Also provide:
- Shorter version for email
- Prevention tip (how to avoid this objection earlier)
```

## ROI Calculator Content

```
Build ROI calculation for:

**Client:** [Company Name]
**Current situation:**
- Hours/week on [task]: [X]
- People involved: [X]
- Hourly cost (fully loaded): $[X]
- Error rate: [X]%
- Cost per error: $[X]
- Additional costs: [list any]

**After automation:**
- Estimated time reduction: [X]%
- Estimated error reduction: [X]%
- Additional benefits: [list any]

Generate:
1. Monthly cost breakdown (current)
2. Monthly cost breakdown (automated)
3. Monthly savings
4. Annual savings
5. Recommended pricing (15-25% of savings)
6. Payback period
7. 3-year ROI projection
8. Conservative/moderate/optimistic scenarios
```

- Main problem: [What they struggled with]

**Results:**

## Upsell Email

```
Write an upsell email for:

**Current client:** [Name at Company]
**Current plan:** [What they have]
**Upsell to:** [What we want to sell]
**Trigger:** [Why now - usage milestone, time-based, new feature, etc.]

Their situation:
- Using for: [What they currently do with us]
- Results so far: [If known]
- Growth indicators: [Signs they need more]

Generate:
1. Subject line (value-focused)
2. Opening that acknowledges their success
3. Bridge to the upsell opportunity
4. Benefits of upgrading (specific to their use)
5. Social proof (similar company that upgraded)
6. Easy next step
7. Alternative: Provide value even if they don't upgrade
```

---

## Tips for Sales Content

1. **Use their words** - Mirror the language from discovery
2. **Lead with outcomes** - Not features
3. **Include specific numbers** - Vague = weak
4. **Create urgency** - But don't be pushy
5. **One CTA per email** - Don't confuse
6. **Social proof close to ask** - Right before CTA
7. **Make it skimmable** - Busy people scan
8. **Follow up persistently** - Most deals need 5+ touches
